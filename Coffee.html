COFFEE BEANS

There are 4 types of coffee bean. Arabica, Robusta, Excelsa and Liberica. All four have radically different taste profiles.

Arabica

Coffea arabica, also known as the Arabic coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the coffee and madder family Rubiaceae. It is believed to be the first species of coffee to have been cultivated and is currently the dominant cultivar, representing about 60% of global production

Robusta

What is Robusta Coffee? Robusta Coffee is coffee made from the beans of the Coffea canephora plant, the origins of which are in Africa. Robusta coffee is notoriously bitter and is used primarily in instant coffee, espresso, and as a filler in certain blends of ground coffee

Excelsa

Excelsa coffee has a distinct and unique taste profile. Simultaneously, these beans contain the tart, fruity characteristics of a lighter roast and the strong, rich profile of a dark roast. This mysterious palette has made it a popular choice for coffee drinkers around the world.

Liberica

Liberica has a strong and unique flavor, with some people saying that you either “love or hate it, with no in between”. Those who love the taste of Liberica coffee appreciate the smokey and chocolatey taste of the coffee, particularly because the taste is accompanied by floral and spicy undertones.
